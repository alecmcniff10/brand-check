Publish a new post

Paste your markdown, fill in the details, and download the ready-to-commit HTML file. No conversion tools, no copy-pasting from templates.

Step 1

Article details

The article headline. Shows up large at the top of the post and on the home page.
Becomes posts/your-slug.html
The subtitle / hook that appears under the title. One or two sentences describing what the article is about — what makes someone want to click. Shows up under the headline on the home page and on the article itself.
Roughly 250 words/minute.
Step 2

Hero photo (optional)

Upload the PNG separately to assets/heroes/ in your repo. Just enter the filename here.

Just the filename of the PNG — no path, no assets/heroes/ prefix. Leave blank to use the default abstract stadium graphic.
A short, plain-English description of what's in the photo. Used by screen readers and search engines.
Only matters if the photo gets cropped badly — e.g. a face or key element gets cut off. Try a different option until it looks right.
Step 3

Article body (markdown)

Paste your full markdown. The first paragraph automatically gets the red drop-cap. Use ## for section headings, > for pull quotes, **bold**, *italic*, and [text](url) for links.

Preview will appear here

Step 4

Generate & commit

  1. Click Download HTML → save the file.
  2. In your GitHub repo: Add file → Upload files, drag the downloaded HTML into posts/. Also drag your hero PNG into assets/heroes/ if you have one.
  3. Open index.html in the GitHub editor. Find the posts-manifest block near the top. Paste the manifest snippet below at the top of the array (right after [), and make sure there's a comma after its closing }.
  4. Commit. Live in ~30 seconds.
